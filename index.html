📜 1. Legal & permissions

  • Threatened Species Protection Act 1995
  • Nature Conservation Act 2002
  • National Parks & Reserves Management Act 2002
  • EPBC Act 1999 (Cth) · Crown Lands Act 1976
    • When is a permit required?

    Collecting from national parks, game reserves, Crown land, state forests, or any reserve under the National Parks and Reserves Management Act 2002 requires a permit. Always confirm before you go.

    Work plan essentials

    Location & access

    Site details, landholder, access notes, changes to environment (fire, nesting)

    Timing

    Species availability, flowering/fruiting season, weather review

    Permits

    Copy attached, permission from Indigenous elders / private landholders if needed

    Tools & PPE

    Pole pruner, secateurs, bags, GPS, gloves, hi-vis, first aid

    Work plan review: before collecting, re‑check conditions (weather, ripening, access). Modify species, equipment or permits accordingly — record changes.

    WHS prior to collecting

    • Complete risk assessment (see matrix below)
    • Wear PPE: boots, glasses, gloves, sunscreen, hi-vis, wet weather gear
    • Clean & maintain tools
    • Notify a responsible person if working alone
    Risk rating matrix (example): Likelihood (1–4) x Consequence (1–4) = risk (Low1 to Extreme16).
    Manual handling M6 Slips/trips → after controls M3

    Collecting seed

    Above 2m: pole pruner + ground sheet, rifle (with permission), ladder, ropes, binoculars.

    MethodExample / tool
    Hand picking / fallen limbsCollect from ground, take advantage of windfall
    Pole pruner / secateursCut small branches, drop onto tarpaulin
    Shake / beat onto sheetsFor dehiscent seed, use hessian bags to catch
    🫐 Fleshy (e.g. Billardiera) 🪵 Dry dehiscent (Leptospermum) 🌾 Dry indehiscent (Poa)

    Sustainability & provenance

    • Collect ≤20% of seed from one plant / population
    • Avoid post‑fire regeneration areas
    • Clean footwear/tools to prevent weed spread
    • Provenance: collect from similar location (rainfall, temp, soil) to planting site → local adaptation

    Clean, store, label

    Cleaning

    • Remove leaves, twigs, chaff
    • Extraction: warm paper bag, low oven, threshing/winnowing, fermentation (fleshy)
    • Dry seeds: spread on plate/tray, turn to reduce moisture

    Storage essentials

    • Fully mature & dried
    • Airtight containers / ziplock (some need fridge)
    • Constant temperature, pest‑proof
    • Watch for mould, mice, boring insects

    minimum label Genus species, date, collector, location (and often easting/northing, # plants).

    Biosecurity & unknowns

    • Clean tools → prevent spread of weeds, pests, diseases.
    • If unsure of species: take voucher sample, photograph, record location details.

    MUST KNOW

    3 main protecting Acts?
    Threatened Species, Nature Conservation, National Parks Mgmt (or EPBC)

    When is permit needed?
    Reserves, State forest, Crown land, National Parks.

    Collection from 2m+?
    Pole pruner, rifle (permit), ladder, ropes

    Ripe & healthy signs?
    Brown/grey woody fruit, no insects/disease, true to type

    Minimal info on label?
    Genus species, date, collector, location

    tools & PPE checklist

  • Pole pruner / secateurs
  • Ground sheets / tarpaulin
  • Bags (paper, hessian, plastic)
  • Ropes / ladders
  • GPS, maps, compass
  • Sieves, tweezers, jars (cleaning)
  • Safety boots, hi‑vis
  • Gloves, glasses, first aid
  • Sunscreen, water, wet‑weather

    • environmental controls

    • avoid disturbing nests
    • max 20% from one plant
    • use clean secateurs – no tearing
    • minimal trampling
